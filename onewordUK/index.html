one.word gives every 2.9 km × 2.9 km square in the UK a unique single-word address drawn from a bank of 171,476 English words.
Why 2.9 km cells?
With only one word, your vocabulary size caps your cell count. To cover the entire UK bounding box without a single duplicate word, the cells must be exactly 0.0262 degrees wide, generating exactly 170,742 cells to fit our word bank perfectly.
How it works
The map uses a strict 1-to-1 sequential mathematical mapping. Every word instantly converts back to an exact X/Y coordinate without any search loops.
