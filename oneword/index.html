one.word gives every 1.2 km × 1.2 km square on Earth a unique single-word address drawn from a bank of 171,476 English words.
Why 1.2 km cells?
what3words covers Earth at 3 m precision using combinations of 3 words (~40k³ ≈ 64 trillion cells). With only one word, the vocabulary size caps how many cells you can have. 171,476 words means cells must each cover roughly ~1.44 km² to tile the entire globe without collisions.
How it works
A fast integer hash of each cell's (column, row) grid coordinates maps deterministically to a word index — no database needed. The same cell always produces the same word, anywhere in the world.
Precision
Suitable for neighbourhoods, parks, and meeting areas. Not precise enough for individual buildings — for that you'd need multiple words.
